Boys' and Girls' Day in Hessen!

Breaking Stereotypes, One Day at a Time

In the German state of Hesse, an exciting and meaningful event is taking place — Boys' and Girls' Day! This annual event gives schoolchildren the chance to explore careers that are traditionally seen as “unusual” for their gender.

On this day, boys can try working in kindergartens, schools, or healthcare settings to see what it’s like to be a teacher, caregiver, or nurse. Meanwhile, girls visit engineering firms, car repair shops, IT companies, or even police departments to discover that technology and hands-on work is for everyone — not just for boys.

We want to show kids that there are no ‘male’ or ‘female’ jobs — only interests and talents, says Maria Schulz, the program coordinator in Hesse. “It helps students discover new possibilities and think more openly about their futures.”

The event has been held across Germany for more than 20 years and is now an important part of school life and career orientation. On this day, students don’t just listen to presentations — they actually get involved: asking questions, trying tasks, and getting hands-on experience.

"I really enjoyed working at a kindergarten. The kids were so sweet! Now I’m thinking maybe I’ll become a teacher,” said 15-year-old David from Frankfurt.

This is how Germany encourages young people to break stereotypes, explore new ideas, and dream boldly about their future — no matter their gender!
